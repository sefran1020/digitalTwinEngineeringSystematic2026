Dear Editor,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript, **"Digital Twins in Engineering and STEM Higher Education: A Systematic Review of Architectures, Pedagogy, and the Quality of Learning Evidence,"** for consideration as a **critical survey (systematic review)** in the *IEEE Transactions on Learning Technologies*.

**Topic and contribution.** Digital twins are increasingly proposed to address the cost, safety, access, and continuity constraints of practical engineering education, yet the educational evidence is dispersed and uneven. Our review synthesises 65 primary studies (2018–2026) across four objectives — technological architecture and deployment environments, pedagogy and Industry 4.0/5.0 competencies, empirical learning outcomes, and institutional benefits and barriers. Its distinctive contribution is a *critical, appraisal* stance: rather than cataloguing applications, we score the methodological quality of the learning evidence (a sensitivity-tested 0–6 composite) and analyse how technical, pedagogical, and evaluative choices co-vary. This exposes a structural decoupling between the technical maturity of the twin and the rigour of its evaluation, and a near-empty "capability-by-evidence" frontier. As Table I documents, no prior review specific to digital twins in education weighs the quality of the learning evidence; doing so, and reading architecture, pedagogy and evidence jointly through an instructional-alignment lens, is what this survey contributes to the learning-technologies community.

**Type of review and methods.** The review follows PRISMA 2020 from a pre-specified protocol. Title/abstract screening combined an automated terminological screen with semantic screening by three role-conditioned large language model reviewers and a moderator agent (inter-agent Fleiss' κ = 0.60), followed by human eligibility review; extraction was theme-conditional with double verification. The protocol, full screening trace, extraction data, and the end-to-end analysis code are openly released for reproducibility.

**Manuscript details.** IEEEtran two-column format, approximately 17 pages; single-paragraph abstract and Index Terms; 11 figures (including a PRISMA flow diagram) and 3 tables; numeric IEEE citations. The full list of the 65 included studies is provided as Supplementary Material.

**Originality and ethics.** This manuscript is original, has not been published previously, and is not under consideration elsewhere. A "Statement on Artificial Intelligence" disclosing our use of AI tools (in screening, document processing, and manuscript drafting) is included, and the corresponding methods are described in the text. All authors have approved the submission and take full responsibility for the content. The authors declare no conflict of interest.
