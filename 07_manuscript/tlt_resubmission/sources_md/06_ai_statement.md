# Statement on Artificial Intelligence

In the interest of transparency, and consistent with the journal's policy, we
disclose the following uses of artificial intelligence (AI) in this work, beyond
the standard copyediting tools embedded in word processors (e.g., grammar
checkers).

*Inductive thematic analysis.* The eight inductive themes (T1–T8) were derived
with three frontier LLMs — ChatGPT 5.5, Gemini 3.1 Pro, and Claude Opus 4.7 —
whose independent codings were iteratively cross-fed and refined under author
review. The resulting theme–objective scheme and all decisions were determined by
the authors; the three models' raw outputs are available in the public repository.

*Data screening.* Three independent large language model (LLM) agents — implemented
with DeepSeek-V4-Flash in a non-reasoning configuration at temperature 0 to ensure
deterministic, reproducible classification — performed the semantic screening of
records against the protocol's pre-specified inclusion/exclusion tests and
construct-severity mapping, each returning a decision, a confidence score, and the
governing criterion; a fourth agent (same model and settings) acted as moderator
for split or uncertain cases. AI-assigned decisions were not treated as final:
inter-agent reliability was quantified (Fleiss' κ = 0.60), all records flagged as
include or uncertain were reviewed by the authors, and the eligibility of every
included study was confirmed by a human. This use is described in the Methods
(Study selection).

*Data processing.* Full-text PDFs were converted to structured text with an
automated document-parsing pipeline (Marker) to support extraction. Every
extracted value was verified by a human against the source and recorded with a
verbatim supporting quotation and its location (Methods, Data extraction). The
analytical results — descriptive profiling, the evidence-quality composite, and
the relational analyses — were produced by an author-developed, reproducible
computational pipeline, not generated by AI.

*Manuscript development.* Generative AI tools were used to assist with drafting
and language refinement of portions of the manuscript. All AI-assisted text was
reviewed, edited, and verified by the authors.

AI tools were not used to generate, alter, or fabricate data; to select,
invent, or format references; or to produce the study's findings. The authors
take full responsibility for the validity, accuracy, and integrity of the entire
manuscript, including all claims, citations, and references.
