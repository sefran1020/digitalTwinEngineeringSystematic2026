TITLE: Digital Twins in Higher Education Engineering and STEM Education: A Systematic Review

DOMAIN: digital_twins_in_engineering_and_stem_education

REVIEW_TYPE: systematic

N_AGENTS: 3

TOPIC_STATEMENT:
Educational use of Digital Twins in higher education engineering and STEM
contexts, covering pedagogical purpose, learning environments, technological
characteristics and reported learning outcomes.

REVIEW_QUESTION:
How are Digital Twins being applied in higher education engineering and STEM
education contexts?

# Decomposes REVIEW_QUESTION to ORIENT the reading/analysis of each abstract.
# TYPE: PICO | PCC | PECO | PEO | SPIDER  (according to the review type).
# References the construct in SCOPE_OF_REVIEW; does NOT redefine it or restate criteria.
QUESTION_FRAMEWORK:
TYPE: PCC
POPULATION: Higher-education / university engineering or STEM students and instructors
CONCEPT: Educational implementation, deployment, evaluation or use of a Digital Twin (construct as defined in SCOPE_OF_REVIEW)
CONTEXT: University-level engineering/STEM courses, laboratories, training or assessment settings
OUTCOME: Reported evidence of learning, competence, engagement, usage or evaluation

# General objective (and specific ones if applicable). Orients the design (Phase 3).
OBJECTIVE:
Characterize how Digital Twins are applied in higher-education engineering and
STEM education: pedagogical purpose, learning environments, technological
characteristics and reported learning outcomes.

SEARCH_STRING:
("digital twin*" OR "educational digital twin*") AND ("engineering education"
OR "engineering training" OR "technical education" OR "STEM education")

# Single canonical source of the construct. Every other key references it.
SCOPE_OF_REVIEW:
Empirical studies (qualitative, quantitative or mixed) conducted in
higher-education / university engineering or STEM settings that implement,
deploy, evaluate or use a Digital Twin for an educational purpose.

CORE CONSTRUCT (single canonical definition; every criterion and agent rule
below refers to THIS and must not redefine it):
A Digital Twin is a virtual representation of a SPECIFIC physical asset,
system or process, bidirectionally and continuously (live or near-live)
coupled to it, so that state changes propagate physical->virtual AND
virtual->physical (feedback/control).

NOT a Digital Twin by itself (near-misses): standalone simulation or
numerical model; virtual or remote laboratory; VR/AR/XR/MR; metaverse;
serious game; a generic CPS/IoT platform or testbed without a synchronized
virtual replica; a "digital model" (no automatic data exchange); a "digital
shadow" (one-way physical->virtual only). A near-miss qualifies ONLY if the
abstract states an explicit Digital Twin with the bidirectional coupling above.

CONSTRUCT SEVERITY (decision mapping; consistent with UNCERTAINTY_HANDLING -
guards against construct inflation / semantic drift):
- STRICT_DT: explicit bidirectional, synchronized physical<->virtual coupling
  -> construct axis CONFIRMED.
- MODERATE_DT: a "digital twin" is claimed but synchronization is only partial
  or unstated -> UNCERTAIN (never exclude).
- NEAR_MISS: the abstract explicitly states a simulation / VR / AR / XR /
  virtual lab / metaverse / generic CPS/IoT system with no synchronized
  coupling -> EXCLUDE (positive disqualifier).

ELIGIBLE_DOCS: original_research_article; conference_paper; early_access_article

INELIGIBLE_DOCS: systematic_review; scoping_review; mapping_study; narrative_review; survey; editorial; opinion_paper; abstract; poster; keynote_summary

# Single source of the document-type gate. Do not restate document types below.
DOCUMENT_RULE:
Hard gate applied BEFORE thematic relevance. Eligible: empirical journal
articles, full conference papers and early-access empirical articles.
Ineligible: any review (systematic, scoping, mapping, narrative), survey,
editorial, opinion paper, abstract, poster or keynote summary. Document type
alone is disqualifying and is judged ONLY here; do not restate it in the
inclusion/exclusion or agent rules.

# Normative "what". Does not redefine the construct or the document gate.
INCLUSION_CRITERIA:
- Higher-education / university-level setting in engineering or STEM
- Implements, deploys, evaluates or uses a Digital Twin (as defined in SCOPE_OF_REVIEW) for an educational purpose
- Empirical study: reports qualitative, quantitative or mixed evidence (e.g. learning outcomes, usage, evaluation or performance data)

# Normative "what". POSITIVE disqualifiers only (something the abstract asserts).
EXCLUSION_CRITERIA:
- The publication type is ineligible per DOCUMENT_RULE (judged solely there, before thematic relevance)
- The abstract explicitly confines the Digital Twin to an industrial/operational use with no educational component
- The abstract explicitly places the study outside higher education (K-12, primary, secondary, or vocational not linked to higher education)
- The abstract explicitly describes a near-miss per SCOPE_OF_REVIEW with no synchronized physical-virtual coupling (exclusion must be stated, not merely unconfirmed)
- Purely technical Digital Twin architecture with no educational application or evaluation
- Duplicate record across databases

# "How" the agent decides: POSITIVE tests decidable from the abstract.
AGENT_INCLUDE_WHEN:
- The abstract gives positive evidence of a Digital Twin as defined in SCOPE_OF_REVIEW (explicit construct plus its discriminating bidirectional coupling)
- An educational use is explicit (teaching, laboratory, training or assessment) in a higher-education engineering/STEM setting
- The study reports empirical data, an evaluation, or learning outcomes

# "How" the agent excludes: each bullet must be something the abstract ASSERTS
# explicitly. Never phrase as "without X" / exclude-by-absence.
AGENT_EXCLUDE_WHEN:
- The abstract itself reveals the item is an ineligible document type as defined in DOCUMENT_RULE
- The abstract explicitly describes a near-miss per SCOPE_OF_REVIEW and explicitly indicates there is no synchronized physical-virtual coupling
- The abstract explicitly limits the Digital Twin to an industrial/operational use with no educational component
- The abstract explicitly places the study outside higher education (K-12, secondary, non-linked vocational)
- The abstract presents only Digital Twin architecture/engineering with no educational application or evaluation

# PRECEDENCE rule (authoritative): overrides the exclusion criteria under ambiguity.
UNCERTAINTY_HANDLING:
Vote "uncertain" whenever title and abstract do not let you POSITIVELY confirm
NOR positively disconfirm any of: (a) a Digital Twin as defined in
SCOPE_OF_REVIEW is implemented or used (vs. a near-miss); (b) the setting is
higher-education engineering/STEM; (c) the evidence is empirical. Absence or
ambiguity of confirming detail is "uncertain", NEVER "exclude". This rule
OVERRIDES the exclusion criteria: "exclude" requires the abstract to positively
state a disqualifying feature and must never be inferred from missing, implied
or partial information. When in doubt, defer to human review.

JUSTIFICATION_STYLE: Concise, evidence-based rationales grounded only in title, abstract and keywords; name the decisive axis (document type, construct, context or empirical evidence) and cite the abstract span that settled the vote.
